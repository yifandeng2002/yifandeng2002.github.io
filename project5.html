Period God
Team
Joe Deng, Xiaoxu Han, Sofia Pace, Niki Hu
Role
Sound Design
Tools
Ableton Live, ICST Ambisonics

Overview

Period God is a sound design project for a motion capture performance showcased at the Spring Show at 370 Jay St’s Audio Lab. It is a 7.1.2 multichannel piece designed and engineered on Ableton Live with E4L.

Implementation

After the team provided the concept, we discussed and agreed that the sound design would be minimal techno with an epic and religious atmosphere, so I used elements such as bells, acid synths, strings, and a deep bass kick drum. Inspired by the theme of “period,” I also added liquid dripping FXs to simulate blood. I created the entire sound design from scratch using Ableton Live. To give each track spatial depth, I used the E4L tool to pan the individual elements, and rendered the final track in multiple formats: 7.1.2, binaural, and stereo, to ensure compatibility with the Audio Lab’s sound system for live performance and possible headphone preview. Listeners can perceive subtle differences between each channel played by its corresponding speaker(s), which is one of the key aspects of the immersive experience provided by the 7.1.4 surround format.
