Product design, Engineering, Fabrication

Overview

Sound Bubbles is a design and research piece that explores how we interact with spatial audio through a fun and tactile interface. This work encourages natural discovery and leads to exploration and understanding of spatial relationships through direct manipulation and active listening.

Features

Sound Bubbles consists of four precision knobs and a multifunctional button. Visitors put on headphones and interact with the controller's four knobs and push button. Three knobs map directly to spatial coordinates (X, Y, Z). Users can also use the iPad's touch screen for control. The fourth knob controls volume, and the button manages sound sources â€“ adding, removing, or selecting between them, allows for adding sounds (double press), switching between them (single press), or removing them (long press). As users manipulate the knobs, they can see their actions reflected visually on both screens while experiencing the sound's movement through the headphones. A brief guide will be provided to help visitors understand the basic interactions while encouraging exploration.

Implementation

The project uses Unity to create an interactive spatial audio environment. It communicates via WiFi with an ESP8266 microcontroller, which acts as a soft access point and reads values from physical knobs. These values are transmitted through UDP to Unity, where they're translated into three-dimensional sound positioning and are visualized afterward.
